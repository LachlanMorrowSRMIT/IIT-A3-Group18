Cybersecurty:

What does it do?

Cybersecurity is the overall concept of protecting different electronic data in servers, and personal computers and the ways we can take actions to achieve this protection. These actions are used to prevent hackers from gaining access to important data and information such as a company’s customers information including credit card details and personal information such as their address.  

There are many threats that cybersecurity deals with, some common one’s include malware, ransomware, and phishing attacks. Malware also known as viruses are software that are created to cause harm this can be done by the user simply clicking a link. Ransomware is a form of malware by as the name suggests the hacker requests a ransom for the company to have access to their systems. Phishing is typically presented as an email such as pretending to be as a bank requesting credit card information.  

To deal with this, cybersecurity includes many technologies that are vital for protecting personal computers to organizations. Some simple technology includes antivirus software which its key role is to detect and remove malicious items on a computer/laptop some antivirus software include Avast, McAfee, and Norton. Another technology used in cybersecurity is DNS filtering which is a technology that allows the user to ensure the domain they are accessing to be free of malicious content, it acts as a filter for different website domains to determine whether it is ‘good’ or ‘bad.’ DNS filtering is also used by organizations to prevent their workers from entering a specific site that could cause cybersecurity threats. 

There are many types of cybersecurity this includes operational security, application security, network security and cloud security.

Operational security consists of a plan for all cybersecurity risks. Usually created by a risk management officer that created a plan to ensure user’s data stored in a company’s server is secure even if an attack occurs. Some security practices used are making sure the employees of a company are well educated about cybersecurity threats. 

Application security consists of the protection for applications we install on our personal devices. Some security practices currently used include a password from a user and using dual factor authentication.  

Network security involves the protection of the network users and company’s use. It ensures only authorized users can access the network and block hackers/attackers. Some of the techniques used to protect against the network include firewalls also network attacks can usually come in the form as a phishing attack sent via email and could pose a risk to a business’s network thus email security is additionally important in network security. 

Cloud security involves the protection of everything we store in the cloud, services such as OneDrive and Google Drive, also the protection for major organizations cloud servers. Some of the security practices used currently include ensuring the user’s network is secure, multiple options to back-up the data stored on the cloud and different encryption techniques.  

All these different areas within cybersecurity are dealt with many professionals within the field including cybersecurity specialists, cybersecurity analysts, cybersecurity engineer and network engineer.  

So right now, cybersecurity can currently do a lot including protecting individual computers and organizations confidential data, but currently it is increasingly difficult for law enforcement to prosecute these cybercrimes. However, what is the future of cybersecurity, currently the employment rate for cybersecurity specialists is not at a high level and not filling the positions required which could change with the current trends in cybersecurity. Some threats are planned to or are in trend include malware automation, polymorphic malware, and third-party software which could contain imbedded malware that could target the company’s system. In addition, the use of Internet of Things threats is continually increasing due to the continuous increase of individuals owning their own personal devices thus, in the future cybersecurity would focus a lot on the Internet of Things attacks. Autonomous Vehicles:

What does it do?

Autonomous Vehicles are vehicles that require no human interactions with the use of sensors and cameras to map out the environment. They require many different technologies to work including sensors, machine learning system, actuators, and different processors to run the software required. An autonomous vehicle is sometimes mixed up with self-driving vehicles, but self-driving vehicles require a human to be always present such as Tesla’s.  

So how do autonomous vehicles work, autonomous vehicles must use sensors to map out the environment they are in. To detect other cars that are within the range of the vehicle it requires specific sensors which are radar sensors. In addition, the use of lidar sensors it allows the car to identify different distances such as the distance of the car in front and provides the ability for the car to detect the lane marks. In addition, the vehicle requires cameras that are externally attached to monitor traffic lights, pedestrians, the different road signs in place, and an addition to radar sensors it also monitors other vehicles.  

Currently autonomous vehicles are not widely used, this is due to different countries regulations and the instability of the technology currently. Some of the key issues include the different weather conditions we experience such as snow making the lanes unclear for the camera and sensor. In addition, when the vehicle does have accidents who is liable for the damage as the passenger has no control. However, autonomous vehicles do have positives with predictions of fuel prices reducing, reduction of traffic congestion in major cities and reducing carbon emission.  

Some autonomous that have been used include the autonomous vehicles used in the 2020 Tokyo Olympics which were used to transport athlete around the Olympic village. However, they were temporarily suspended after a vehicle hit a visually impaired athlete. Which as highlighted earlier the current issues of autonomous cars. Raspberry Pis, Arduinos, Makey Makeys and other small computing devices:

What does it do?

Throughout the recent years, there have been many “emerging small computing devices that provide an accessible and easy way for software developers” [10] to start learning how to use various pieces of hardware to collect inputs and produce outputs. These computing devices include Arduino boards, Raspberry Pis and a variety of other “beginner hardware invention kits” [11]. Arduino is an open-source electronics platform which utilizes various hardware components including a single-board microcontroller [12]. The main reason Arduino is so easy to learn is due to the large community surrounding it, this results in a large knowledge base and abundance of resources that beginners can draw from when learning the platform (What is Arduino?, 2018). Raspberry Pi on the other hand is a “microprocessor-based minicomputer” [13] which usually runs using a Linux based OS. In comparison to Arduino, a Raspberry Pi can “run multiple programs at once” [14] making it better suited to more complex projects partly also due to its “better processor allowing for more complex computations to take place” [14]. Many other small computing devices perform similar functions to both Raspberries Pis and Arduinos such as BeagleBoard, Sharks Cove, Nanode etc. However, Arduinos and Raspberries Pis are by far the most widely used and recognised ones due to both ease of use and accessibility.

The applications for both Arduinos and Raspberry Pis are quite endless as they can be used in all sorts of DIY projects [15] from home automation to simple robotics. This is achieved through various sensors and modules [17] that can be used in conjunction with them to either detect inputs or perform an output. One example of an individual who largely utilizes these devices to create interesting projects is Youtuber Michael Reeves who often uses both Arduinos and Raspberry Pis to program a wide variety of robotics. An example is when he made a Roomba that screams when bumping into various objects [19], this was achieved using a Raspberry Pi with a couple of optical sensors and a Bluetooth speaker. This shows how these devices can be utilized to produce output when certain inputs are detected by sensors for a wide variety of applications [15]. Also, these devices allow people not familiar with the hardware aspect of programming to become exposed to the hardware and start learning how to use it to complete a wide variety of tasks. This allows them to build their knowledge using these beginner systems until they are comfortable enough to take what they have learned and apply it to the more complex hardware used in the industry.

In the future, these devices are expected to be able to continue to lower the cost of development using minicomputer and microcontrollers to make them more accessible to both hobbyists [18] but also give developing countries access to these computing devices allowing them to modernise. This could include the implementation of “low-cost PLC controllers” for example which will help industrial automation [20] reducing the amount of physical labour required in third world countries and help them localize manufacturing. Additionally, Arduino in the future once it becomes more efficient is going to allow companies to make products that are more easily customizable by consumers. For example, if someone buys a product and wishes it had an additional feature or if a current feature acted differently, they could take it into their own hands and program the new feature themselves. This would be possible due to the open-source nature of Arduino meaning that it acts the same across multiple products.

Blockchain and Cryptocurrencies:

What does it do?

Blockchain is essentially a “distributed ledger” [21] which is a system that records the transaction details of assets [22]. This data is recorded across many devices which are also referred to as nodes. These nodes are used to validate each transaction before storing them in blocks [23]. Each block is a group of data of transactions with each block having a capacity on the amount of data that can be stored this results in the addition of more blocks therefore leading to a ‘blockchain’[21]. Blocks all have a cryptographic hash which uniquely identifies each block and prevents from others seeing the data stored on a block [24] by assigning a code of a fixed size to the block using a hash algorithm. This hash cannot be decrypted. It also ensures that each block has its own cryptographic hash securing each block's place relative to each other in the blockchain. All nodes on the blockchain keep track of the data stored on the blockchain across a period of time, additionally when data is stored on the blockchain it is permanent and cannot be altered. Due to the transaction history of data being recorded over time, it allows for the “ownership history of any digital asset” to be readily available [25].

Cryptocurrencies are digital currencies which utilize blockchain technology examples of these are Bitcoin Ethereum, Dogecoin etc. Due to using blockchain technology all transactions of these cryptocurrencies are recorded and very secured. Cryptocurrencies are like any other currency in that they can be used to make a variety of purchases from whoever accepts them as payment [26]. As cryptocurrencies especially bitcoin have grown more popular over the last decade more and more companies even governments are starting to recognise bitcoin as a legitimate payment method. Another benefit of cryptocurrencies is that they exist on a decentralised system this means that they can be sent to any country without requiring any currency conversions and are kept by the user, not an external entity such as a bank. One downside of cryptocurrencies however, is the extreme volatility associated with them, this is due to the large amount of speculation surrounding their usefulness from the public resulting in both large price surges and major drops.

Another aspect of blockchain technology related to cryptocurrency is miners. They are people involved in the creation of new blocks via mining [25]. This is achieved by using high-level computers to solve very complicated mathematical equations. Solving these equations is essentially verifying each transaction made on the blockchain [26]. Miners who are the first to have either the equal or closest answer will be paid in the corresponding cryptocurrency for example those mining bitcoin would receive a certain amount of bitcoin.

Ethereum uses “traditional blockchain functionality” [25] however it also allows for computer code to be executed on the blockchain. This results in programs being able to communicate through the blockchain. These programs can then use tokens which can represent any digital asset and because this is done via blockchain technology anyone can track ownership of the particular asset. These tokens lead to newly popularised NFTs (Non-Fungible-Tokens) which are tokens that can store art, music and a variety of other digital files. Creators have an option to take a percentage of any sales made involving the NFT allowing them to benefit if it balloons in price. Many companies and mainstream artists are starting to get into the NFT market due to how the absurd prices they can go for. For example, Grimes a music artist sold a video named “Death of the Old” that went for $389,000 [27]. Some have taken it a step further using NFT to sell player virtual land plots and characters in blockchain-based videogames.
